How to play Teen Do Paanch

About Game
3-2-5 Bridge commonly known as Teen Do Paanch or Do Teen Paanch is one of the most basic household games played in India. It is a 3 player-30 standard cards-10 tricks continuous game where the winner of the previous turn gets advantage of withdrawing cards from the loser's cards in the current game.

Setting Trump
At the start of every round of game, five cards are distributed. The player who has to make 5 tricks chooses the trump out of the four suits. Once, the trump is set the remaining five cards are distributed to each player. The trump is then visible to everyone from the very beginning.
The trump functions the usual way as in any card game. If you do not have the required suit of a turn, you have the option to use your trump and claim the hand. Of course, if the next player uses a bigger trump, he'll own the trick. A turn can also start by playing the trump.
Game Play
The player setting the trump starts the game. We'll call the suit of the first card played in a turn, the turn-suit.

The next two players have to play a card of the turn-suit. If they do not have any turn-suit, they can choose either to play a trump or a card of another suit.

The player with the largest card of the turn-suit wins the trick, or in case a trump was played, the player with the largest trump wins. The trick winner then plays the next card.

The game round continues in the same manner for the next 10 hands. When the next round starts, hands made for each player are checked against the hands each one had to make.

At the beginning of the next round, the player with extra hands can withdraw cards from the players with hand-deficit.
Next Round: Withdraw and Return
After all the cards are distributed and the trump for the next round is set, the player with extra hands gets a chance to withdraw cards from the players with hand deficit. The player who's withdrawing, picks a random card from the other player's deck and then can return the card of his choice back. He also has an option to return back the card he had withdrawn.

3-2-5 Bridge Variations
Like any other card game, 3-2-5 Bridge has its own variations. We discussed the simplest gameplay above. However, introducing variations makes it all the more enjoyable. We'll discuss a few variations here. Well most of them are from the Wikipedia page.
  • Dealing the cards: If the player choosing the trump does not like his/her first 5 cards, he/she may decide to ask the next set of 3 cards to be shown to all and the suit with the highest card in those 3 becomes the trump.

  • Trumps: The 7 of spades and the 7 of hearts are sometimes not considered as trumps at all.

  • Return after withdrawal: While returning cards to other players at the start of a next round, one cannot return and thus become devoid of a suit. The person during gameplay has to show at least one card of the suit that the returned.
